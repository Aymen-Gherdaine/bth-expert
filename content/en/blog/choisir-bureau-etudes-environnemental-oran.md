Two identical files on paper, submitted a few weeks apart to the same Wilaya Department of the Environment, can meet radically different fates: one processed without any request for additional information, the other returned with a request for supplementary documents that adds two months to the schedule. The difference rarely comes down to luck — it comes down to the consultancy that prepared the file. Here are the criteria that make a real difference.

## 1. Ministerial Accreditation: A Non-Negotiable Requirement

In Algeria, only consultancies accredited by the Ministry of Environment and Quality of Life are authorised to produce [EIE](/en/services/etude-impact-environnemental) and [EDD](/en/services/etude-de-dangers) reports that are admissible by the administration. Before any technical or commercial discussion, verify that the consultancy's accreditation is valid and specifically covers the type of study your project requires — accreditation for an EIE does not automatically cover an EDD.

## 2. In-Depth Knowledge of the Regulatory Framework Applicable to Your Sector

A generalist consultancy that does not master sector-specific requirements — [executive decree no. 07-145 for the EIE](/en/blog/etude-impact-environnemental-obligatoire-algerie), [the authorisation and declaration regimes under decree 06-198 for classified establishments](/en/blog/etude-de-dangers-etablissements-classes-algerie), APR/HAZOP methods for risk analysis — will produce a file that is theoretically complete but ill-suited to the actual expectations of the Wilaya Department of the Environment (DEW) or the Directorate of Industry and Mines (DIM). The result: requests for additional information that extend timelines by several weeks or even several months.

## 3. A Transparent Methodology, from Fieldwork to Report

A good consultancy explains its approach before billing for it: initial site assessment, impact or hazard analysis, mitigation or risk control measures, monitoring programme. If this methodology is not formalised from the quotation stage — with intermediate deliverables and associated milestones — it is a warning sign regarding the rigour of the final deliverable.

## 4. Geographical Proximity and Knowledge of the Algerian Context

A reliable initial site assessment requires on-site surveys, not solely bibliographic data. A consultancy established in Algeria — and ideally in the project's region — is familiar with local specificities (soils, hydrology, environmental sensitivities particular to the wilaya) and can respond quickly when the administration requests additional information. For a project in western Algeria, a consultancy based in Oran has an on-site response time that a consultancy operating solely from Algiers cannot guarantee.

## 5. Clearly Committed Timelines

The cost of an authorisation delay far exceeds the cost of the study itself: a construction site that starts two months late has a financial impact that is rarely measured in tens of thousands of dinars. Require a precise schedule, from initial scoping to submission of the report to the competent authorities, and check the consultancy's references on [projects in sectors comparable to yours](/en/secteurs).

## In Summary

Valid accreditation, sector expertise, transparent methodology, local presence, and met deadlines: these are the five criteria that distinguish a reliable consultancy from a mere administrative service provider. A well-prepared file rarely costs more than a rushed one — it simply avoids the detours.
